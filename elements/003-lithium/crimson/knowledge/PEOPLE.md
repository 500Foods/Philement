# People

The Philement project and its subprojects like Hydrogen, Helium, Lithium, and so on are an open-source project first conceived in mid-2024 by Andrew Simard. 
This started out initially as a desire to replace the Klipper software in his new Troodon 2.0 Pro 3D printer. Fuelled mostly due to a seething dislike for Python.
Scope creep quickly set in and this became a platform for a great many other projects having nothing at all to do with Kilppper or 3D printing. Its modular
structure and compact and efficient design have provided useful as a platform for general web development for traditianal apps like CRM and ERP-style 
client-server apps. 

## About Andrew Simard

Andrew got his start in computers first in elementary school on the ubiquitous Apple ][ while toiling away at home on the far less expensive Commodore VIC-20.
Junior High brought new adventures with the Commodore 64, where he wrote a "Library Overdues" program for the school librarian. High school saw the transition
to the PC environment starting with the very noble Turbo Pascal 4. As part of a class team project, he worked on a program for the school district's Track and
Field event, used to collect and post event scores.

In university, the first computers used were NeXT computers, of all things. An interest in databases, operating systems, and computer graphics quickly developed
that has persisted to this day as can be seen directly in some of the current project work. In particular, a 3rd-year computer graphics course introduced him to
Lindenmayer Systems, something that has been in his thoughts and schemes ever since, even 30 years later.

While in university, one co-op job introduced him to the wonders of IBM Mainframes and DB2. Particularly impactful at the time, and likely the reason why DB2 is 
one of the default supported databases in the project today. Another co-op involved working with the federal government - what was the Weather Office of 
Environment Canada at the time. The exposure to government bureaucracy and general office politics is likely a major contributing factor in his choice to 
remain self-employed for the remainder of his career.

Post-university and co-op jobs, consulting work lead to working with greenhouse businesses. A good fit at first glance, given their lack of technology and many
applications for it in their organizations. Over time this led to the development of a major client-server application, generally referred to as GLM, which is
one of the "modules" that has migrated into the Philement project. This is very much the standard client-server app that has become prevalent everywhere.
